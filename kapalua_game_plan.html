⚠ Trade Wind Protocol — Critical at Kapalua: Kapalua's persistent NE trade winds typically blow 15–25 mph, often gusting to 35+. Into-wind shots lose 10–30 yards; downwind shots gain 20–40 yards. Add 1 club minimum into the wind. Use the mountain slopes and terrain to your advantage — the course was designed to use the land's kicks and funnels. Every club recommendation below assumes calm conditions — adjust aggressively for wind direction on each hole.

The Opener
HCP #1 Downhill Wind L→R
Tee Shot
Driver. Shape right-to-left to find the speed slot. Land ~240yds in left-center fairway. Avoid bunkers that were moved closer to the fairway in 2019 renovation.
Landing Zone
Left of center at ~240yds. A speed slot here gathers the ball on a rare flat spot and leaves ~280yds to the elevated green.
Approach
With ~270–280yds left after a good drive, lay up with 4-Hybrid to ~100yds, then Gap Wedge in. Green is elevated, kicks shots left-to-right.
Green
Expanded elevated green, slopes left-to-right. Multiple bunkers guard front. Aim for center to avoid being above the hole.
⚠ AvoidRight side on approach — trouble lurks up the right. Bunkers guard the entry. Three-putt danger on first green of the day — play for center.

Before You Tee Off

5 Keys to Playing Kapalua Well

01 — Wind is Everything

Add 1–2 clubs into the trade winds and take off 1–2 downwind. Every single club decision on this course is filtered through wind direction first.

02 — Use the Slopes

Coore & Crenshaw cut this course from a mountain. The terrain is your ally. Speed slots, kick slopes, and funnels exist on nearly every hole — read them and let the land do the work.

03 — Stay Below the Hole

The greens run fast back-to-front. Being above the hole on your putt is the fastest way to make bogey or worse. Always aim to leave yourself an uphill putt.

04 — Avoid Three-Putts

These greens are enormous. A missed green is often better than a 60ft first putt. Prioritize approach distance control over hitting the center of massive greens.

05 — Score on the Right Holes

Attack on holes 10, 11, 14, and 16. Survive holes 1, 8, 9, and 17. Bogey is not a disaster — protect your score on the brutal holes and birdie the gettable ones.

Bonus — Your Driver is a Weapon

At 250yds off the tee on a course with wide fairways, you have an advantage. Use it everywhere except where precision is required (holes 5 right side, 8 carry). Wide fairways mean swing freely.
